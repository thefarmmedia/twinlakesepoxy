Mountain Home's Epoxy Garage Floor Contractor

If you're searching for epoxy garage floor coatings in Mountain Home, Bull Shoals, or anywhere across the Twin Lakes Area — you've found the right company. Twin Lakes Epoxy has built its reputation on one thing: doing it right, every time.

We use a professional-grade polyaspartic/polyurea hybrid system — not the standard epoxy you'll find at Home Depot or from most low-cost competitors. Our coating system is six times stronger than traditional epoxy, UV stable, and backed by a written lifetime warranty on every single install.

Why Our Floors Don't Peel The #1 reason garage floors fail is poor surface preparation. Most cheap installers use acid etching or skip prep entirely. We use full diamond grinding on every job — creating the mechanical profile your coating needs for a permanent bond. No exceptions, no shortcuts.

What's Included in Every Garage Floor Install

  • Full diamond grinding surface preparation — standard on every job
  • Crack filling and joint repair before any coating is applied
  • Professional primer coat for maximum adhesion
  • High-performance polyaspartic base coat
  • Full broadcast decorative flake system (30+ color options)
  • UV-stable, chemical-resistant polyaspartic topcoat
  • Written lifetime warranty on every install
  • Complete cleanup — we leave your garage spotless

Why Polyaspartic Beats Regular Epoxy

Most homeowners search for "epoxy garage floors" — totally fine. But what we actually install is a polyaspartic/polyurea hybrid system, which outperforms traditional epoxy in every measurable way.

  • UV Stability: Standard epoxy yellows. Our polyaspartic is fully UV stable — color stays true for 15+ years.
  • Temperature Flexibility: Ozark freeze-thaw cycles crack traditional epoxy. Polyaspartic moves with your concrete.
  • Cure Speed: Drive on it in 24 hours. Traditional epoxy takes 3–7 days.
  • Strength: Up to 6× stronger abrasion resistance — critical for hot tire pickup.
  • Chemical Resistance: Oil, gas, brake fluid, degreasers — our topcoat resists them all.

Garage Floor FAQ

Common Questions

How much does an epoxy garage floor cost?▾
Pricing depends on square footage, floor condition, and system chosen. A typical 2-car garage (400–500 sq ft) commonly runs $1,800–$3,000. Request a free, no-obligation estimate for exact pricing on your space.
How long does the install take?▾
Most garage floors are completed in a single day, start to finish. Larger or heavily damaged floors may take slightly longer.
How long before I can drive on it?▾
Foot traffic within hours, vehicle traffic within 24 hours thanks to our fast-curing polyaspartic system.
Do you repair cracks first?▾
Yes. All cracks and surface damage are filled and leveled as part of our standard prep — we fix problems, not coat over them.
