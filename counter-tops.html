Showroom Looks Without the Stone Price Tag

Epoxy countertops give you the seamless, high-end look of poured stone or resin art at a fraction of the cost of granite or quartz. Whether you want a dramatic metallic pour over your existing counters or a full custom build, Twin Lakes Epoxy handles every step — right here in West Plains and across our 75-mile service area.

Because there are no seams and no grout lines, epoxy countertops are one of the easiest surfaces in your home to keep clean. They resist stains from wine, coffee, and cooking oils, and stand up to daily kitchen abuse far better than laminate.

Resurface, Don't Replace In most cases we can coat directly over your existing laminate, tile, or concrete countertops — no demolition required. That means a dramatic transformation without the cost or mess of a full countertop replacement.

What's Included in Every Countertop Install

  • Surface prep and cleaning of existing counters
  • Repair of chips, cracks, or uneven seams before coating
  • Custom color and pigment selection consultation
  • Professional pour or brush-applied epoxy system
  • Food-safe, heat-resistant topcoat sealer
  • Written lifetime warranty against yellowing or cracking

Where Epoxy Countertops Work Best

Kitchen counters and islands, bar tops, bathroom vanities, outdoor kitchen surfaces, and even reception desks or tabletops for a business. If it's a flat surface that takes daily wear, epoxy is worth considering.

Countertop FAQ

Common Questions

Is epoxy safe for kitchen countertops?▾
Yes. Once fully cured, our epoxy topcoat is food-safe, non-porous, and easy to sanitize — a great surface for daily food prep.
Can you coat over my existing counters?▾
In most cases, yes. We can coat directly over laminate, tile, or existing solid surface counters after proper prep, avoiding the cost and mess of a full tear-out.
How hot can epoxy countertops get before damage?▾
Our topcoat systems resist everyday heat exposure well, but we still recommend trivets or hot pads under pans straight off the stove, just as you would with granite or quartz.
How long until I can use my counters again?▾
Light use is typically fine within 24–48 hours, with full cure for heavy daily use around 3–5 days depending on the system used.
