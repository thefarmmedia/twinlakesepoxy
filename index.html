How It Works

Our Proven 5-Step Process

Every install follows the same meticulous process. No shortcuts. No skipped steps. That's why our floors last 15+ years.

1
Surface Prep

Diamond grinding removes all coatings, oils, and imperfections. Cracks filled and leveled. This step is everything.

2
Primer Coat

Specialized primer applied for maximum adhesion between the coating system and your concrete substrate.

3
Base Coat

High-performance polyaspartic base coat spread evenly across the entire surface creating a flawless foundation.

4
Decorative Layer

Color flakes, metallic pigments, or solid tones broadcast into the wet base to create your custom, unique look.

5
Topcoat Seal

UV-stable, chemical-resistant topcoat locks it all in. The result: a surface that's gorgeous and maintenance-free for 15 years.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked
Questions

How long does an epoxy garage floor last?▾
With professional-grade polyaspartic coatings and full diamond grinding prep, expect 15–25 years of durability. Our system is backed by a written lifetime warranty against fading, peeling, and yellowing.
Do you really coat enclosed trailer floors?▾
Yes — it's one of our specialties. Cargo trailer, car hauler, and concession trailer floors take a beating from moisture, chemicals, and constant loading. Our coating system holds up far better than bare wood, paint, or peel-and-stick options.
Are epoxy countertops durable enough for a kitchen?▾
Absolutely. Our epoxy countertop system is heat resistant, stain resistant, and non-porous — easier to keep clean than butcher block or laminate, at a fraction of the cost of natural stone.
How long before I can drive on my new floor?▾
Because we use polyaspartic coatings, your floor is typically ready for foot traffic within hours and vehicle traffic within 24 hours. Most jobs are done in a single day start to finish.
Why do some epoxy floors peel and fail?▾
The #1 cause of floor failure is skipping or cutting corners on surface preparation. Most cheap installers skip diamond grinding or use acid etching, which doesn't create enough profile for proper adhesion. We use full diamond grinding on every job — no exceptions, ever.
How far do you travel for jobs?▾
We're based in West Plains, MO and serve a full 75-mile radius — including Willow Springs, Mountain View, Ava, Thayer, Cabool, Houston, and across the state line into the Twin Lakes area of Arkansas around Mountain Home, Bull Shoals, and Flippin.
